1f:T9a52,<h2>What is a Lucky 31 and how does the calculator work?</h2>
<p>A Lucky 31 is a full-cover bet with singles on five selections. It creates 31 separate lines: every possible single, double, treble, fourfold and the fivefold accumulator. A calculator processes each line independently based on your stake per line, odds format (fractional or decimal), and the result of each selection (win, lose, or void/non-runner). It then sums all winning line returns and shows total return, total stake, and profit or loss. Voids reduce affected multiples (treating the voided leg at odds of 1.00), while losers make that specific line return zero. If placed each-way, the calculator duplicates all lines into win and place parts and applies the relevant place terms.</p>
<h3>Bet breakdown: 31 lines from singles to fivefold</h3>
<p>A Lucky 31 uses five selections to create every combination from singles to the fivefold. The line count is fixed and does not change with stake size.</p>
<ul><li>Singles: 5 lines (one on each selection)</li><li>Doubles: 10 lines (all two-selection combos)</li><li>Trebles: 10 lines (all three-selection combos)</li><li>Fourfolds: 5 lines (all four-selection combos)</li><li>Fivefold: 1 line (all five selections together)</li><li>Total: 31 lines</li></ul>

<h3>How are returns calculated for winners, losers, and voids?</h3>
<p>Each line is settled independently and then summed. For a win-only Lucky 31 with unit stake s per line:</p>
<ul><li>Convert odds to decimal if needed. A void single returns the stake.</li><li>Total return = sum of all line returns. Profit/Loss = Total return − (31 × s).</li></ul>

<h3>How does each-way change win and place returns?</h3>
<p>Each-way (EW) doubles a Lucky 31 to 62 lines: 31 win lines and 31 place lines. Your total stake doubles because you are placing two bets on each line. The win part uses full win odds; the place part uses reduced place odds based on the market’s place terms (for example, 1/5 or 1/4 of the win odds, with the number of places determined by the event rules). A winner also counts as “placed” for the place part.</p>
<ul><li>Stake: An EW Lucky 31 at s per line stakes 62 × s in total.</li></ul>
<p>Place odds conversion:</p>
<ul><li>Fractional: (a/b) at place fraction f becomes (a × f) / b.</li><li>Decimal: place_decimal = 1 + (win_decimal − 1) × f.</li></ul>
<p>Settlement:</p>
<ul><li>Win part: every leg in that line must win to return.</li><li>Place part: every leg in that line must finish in a place (winners count as placed) to return.</li><li>Voids: treated as odds 1.00 on both win and place parts (the multiple reduces).</li></ul>
<p>Example A — each-way single (£1 EW, total £2 stake), selection @ 9/2 with 1/5 place terms:</p>

<h2>Using the Lucky 31 Calculator: a quick guide</h2>
<p>This guide shows exactly how to input your five selections, choose odds formats, set outcomes, and apply each-way and Rule 4 deductions so you can see total return, total stake, and profit for a Lucky 31. The calculator treats each of the 31 lines independently, applies your settings, and aggregates the results with standard UK settlement conventions and two-decimal rounding.</p>
<h3>Enter odds, results, and each-way or Rule 4 deductions</h3>
<p>Follow these steps to set up and settle a Lucky 31 correctly. The same inputs are applied consistently to every affected line of the bet.</p>
<ul><li>Select odds format (fractional, decimal, or American) and stake per line. Remember, a win-only Lucky 31 has 31 lines; each-way doubles to 62 lines.</li></ul>
<p>For each of the five selections, enter the odds and choose a result:</p>
<ul><li>Win: the selection won (for EW, this also counts as placed).</li><li>Lose: the selection lost (that line returns £0 on the relevant part).</li><li>Void/Non-runner: treated at decimal 1.00; multiples reduce, single returns stake.</li><li>Dead-heat (if provided): the calculator applies the standard reduction by the number of tied runners for the winning/place portion.</li></ul>
<ul><li>Toggle Each-Way (optional). If on, enter the place terms (e.g., 1/5 or 1/4) applicable to the market. The calculator will duplicate all 31 lines into win and place parts and apply reduced place odds to the place part.</li></ul>
<p>Apply Rule 4 deductions (only if your race had a late withdrawal). Enter the deduction as pence in the pound (e.g., 20p = 0.20). The Rule 4 deduction reduces the winnings portion only:</p>
<ul><li>Adjusted decimal for settlement = 1 + (original_decimal − 1) × (1 − deduction).</li><li>Adjusted fractional profit = original_fraction × (1 − deduction).</li><li>Example: 4/1 with a 20p Rule 4 becomes profit odds 4 × 0.8 = 3.2 (decimal 4.20).</li></ul>
<p>Review totals:</p>
<ul><li>Total stake = lines × stake per line (31×s for win-only; 62×s for EW).</li><li>Total return = sum of all winning line returns (after deductions and place terms).</li><li>Profit/Loss = Total return − Total stake.</li></ul>
<h3>Can I use fractional, decimal, or American odds?</h3>
<p>Yes. You can enter odds in fractional (UK), decimal (EU), or American (US) formats. The calculator converts them internally for accurate settlement. Use these standard conversions and probability checks to verify your inputs:</p>
<ul><li>Fractional a/b to decimal: 1 + a/b. Implied probability = b / (a + b).</li><li>Decimal d to American: if d ≥ 2.00, +100 × (d − 1); if d &lt; 2.00, −100 ÷ (d − 1).</li><li>American +X to decimal: 1 + X/100; American −Y to decimal: 1 + 100/Y.</li><li>Implied probability from decimal: 1 ÷ d.</li></ul>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>
<h3>How do dead-heats affect a Lucky 31 settlement?</h3>
<p>In a dead-heat for a winning position, the affected line’s stake is divided by the number of tied runners and the odds stay the same; for place markets, if H horses tie for the final K payable places, the effective stake is multiplied by K/H. Example (win dead-heat): £2 single at 4/1 in a two-way dead-heat returns (2 ÷ 2) × (4 + 1) = £5. Example (place dead-heat): with 1/5 terms and three tied for the final one payable place, a £2 place stake is treated as £2 × (1 ÷ 3) at the place odds; if the place decimal was 1.80, the return is (2 ÷ 3) × 1.80 = £1.20. In multiples, the dead-heat factor simply scales that leg’s contribution: a two-way win dead-heat multiplies the line’s return by 1/2, while a three-for-two tie on the last paying place multiplies it by 2/3.</p>
<h3>How are multiple Rule 4 deductions applied across different legs in a Lucky 31?</h3>
<p>Each affected leg is adjusted independently on its profit portion before combining lines. If a leg has decimal d and a deduction r (e.g., 0.20 for 20p), its adjusted decimal is 1 + (d − 1) × (1 − r). Worked example: Leg A 7/2 (decimal 4.50) with 20p Rule 4 becomes 1 + (4.50 − 1) × 0.80 = 3.80; Leg C 3/1 (4.00) with 10p becomes 1 + 3 × 0.90 = 3.70. A double using A and C settles at 3.80 × 3.70 = 14.06 per £1 on that line. Legs without deductions remain unchanged.</p>
<h3>In what order should I apply place terms, Rule 4, and dead-heat adjustments?</h3>
<p>Apply odds transformations in this sequence on each affected leg: calculate place odds if each-way, then apply Rule 4 to the profit portion, then apply dead-heat reduction to the effective stake/return factor. Micro-case: Win odds 9/2 (decimal 5.50) with 1/5 place terms and 20p Rule 4, dead-heat two-way for first. Place decimal first: 1 + (5.50 − 1) × 0.2 = 1.90. Apply Rule 4 on profit: win decimal becomes 1 + 4.50 × 0.8 = 4.60; place decimal becomes 1 + 0.90 × 0.8 = 1.72. Apply dead-heat factor 1/2 to the relevant part(s): a win dead-heat halves the win-part contribution; a place dead-heat halves the place-part contribution.</p>
<h3>What happens to returns if two or more selections are void in a Lucky 31?</h3>
<p>Voids reduce the affected legs to decimal 1.00 in every line they appear, so combinations compress to smaller multiples and void singles simply return the unit stake. If two legs are void and three run, all lines effectively settle as if built from the three remaining selections: you’ll receive stake back on the two void singles, reduced multiples wherever a void leg appeared, and full settlement on combinations using only the three runners. If all five are void, each line returns exactly the unit stake and total profit is zero.</p>
<h3>Can a Lucky 31 be placed with free bet (stake not returned) funds and how is it settled?</h3>
<p>If a line uses a stake-not-returned fund, its first winning leg contributes profit only; the initial stake is excluded from the rollover. For a k-leg winning line with decimals d1…dk and free stake s, return equals s × (d1 − 1) × d2 × … × dk. Example: free £1 on a double 4.00 and 3.00 pays £1 × (4 − 1) × 3 = £9; the equivalent cash stake would have paid £12.</p>

<h3>How can I compute expected value (EV) for a Lucky 31 with my own probabilities?</h3>
