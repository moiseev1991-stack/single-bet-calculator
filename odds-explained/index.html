Betting Odds Explained

Betting odds tell you two things: how likely the bookmaker thinks an outcome is, and how much you stand to win. There are three main formats — fractional, decimal, and American. All three convey the same information, just expressed differently.

Fractional Odds

The traditional UK format. The first number (numerator) is your profit; the second (denominator) is the stake required. Formula: profit = stake × (numerator ÷ denominator)

Odds £10 stake Profit Total Return
1/1 (Evens) £10 £10.00 £20.00
2/1 £10 £20.00 £30.00
5/2 £10 £25.00 £35.00
1/2 £10 £5.00 £15.00
7/4 £10 £17.50 £27.50

Decimal Odds

The European standard — and the easiest format to calculate with. Decimal odds represent your total return (including stake) per unit staked. Formula: total return = stake × decimal odds; profit = return − stake

Odds £10 stake Total Return Profit
1.50 £10 £15.00 £5.00
2.00 £10 £20.00 £10.00
3.00 £10 £30.00 £20.00
5.00 £10 £50.00 £40.00
10.00 £10 £100.00 £90.00

American (Moneyline) Odds

Used primarily in the USA. There are two cases:

  • Positive (+200): profit per £100 staked. Profit = stake × (X ÷ 100)
  • Negative (−150): stake needed to profit £100. Profit = stake × (100 ÷ Y)
Odds £10 stake Profit Total Return
+100 £10 £10.00 £20.00
+200 £10 £20.00 £30.00
+500 £10 £50.00 £60.00
−150 £10 £6.67 £16.67
−200 £10 £5.00 £15.00

Which Format Should I Use?

  • UK & Ireland — fractional is the traditional format, still widely used by UK bookmakers
  • Europe & Australia — decimal is the standard; easier for multi-bet maths
  • USA — American (moneyline) is universal; all major US sportsbooks use it
